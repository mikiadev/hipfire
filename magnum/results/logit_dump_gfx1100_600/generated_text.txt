### 1. Intake Stroke (Suction)
During the intake stroke, the piston moves down, creating a vacuum. This draws the air-fuel mixture into the cylinder.

### 2. Compression Stroke
During the compression stroke, the piston moves up, compressing the air-fuel mixture.

### 3. Combustion (Power) Stroke
During the combustion stroke, the fuel is ignited. This explosion pushes the piston down.

### 4. Exhaust Stroke
During the exhaust stroke, the piston moves up, pushing the exhaust gases out of the cylinder.

**Fuel Injection**
In modern engines, fuel is injected directly into the cylinder rather than being mixed with air beforehand. This allows for precise control over the amount of fuel burned.

**Exhaust**
The exhaust stroke clears the cylinder of the burnt gases. This prepares the cylinder for the next cycle.
